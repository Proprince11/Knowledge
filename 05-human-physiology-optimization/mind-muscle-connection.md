# Mind-Muscle Connection & Motor Control

The "mind-muscle connection" (MMC) is **attentional focus on contracting a target muscle** — an
*internal* attentional focus. It is a real, measurable neuromuscular phenomenon (elevated
surface-EMG in the targeted muscle when consciously emphasized), and the evidence (Schoenfeld,
Calatayud) suggests it can bias hypertrophy toward the intended muscle at **low-to-moderate
loads** — but it can *reduce* performance when maximal force/output is the goal.

## 1. Technical Mechanisms
- **Motor unit recruitment (Henneman's size principle):** the CNS recruits motor units from
  small→large as force demand rises. Attention + adequate load drives high-threshold recruitment
  in the target.
- **Internal vs. external focus:** *internal* ("squeeze the pec") raises target-muscle EMG and
  aids hypertrophy intent; *external* ("push the floor away / drive the bar") improves force,
  speed, and skill (Wulf's constrained-action hypothesis — internal focus can disrupt automatic
  motor control). **Use internal focus for bodybuilding isolation; external focus for
  strength/power/skill.**
- **Neural skill:** MMC is trainable. Beginners often can't "feel" a muscle (e.g., lats, glutes)
  because motor patterns are unrefined; deliberate practice builds the cortical map.

## 2. Application Frameworks
- **When MMC helps:** isolation work, smaller/lagging muscles, light-moderate loads, controlled
  tempo. **When to drop it:** near-maximal lifts, explosive/athletic movement — go external.
- **Building it:** pre-activation (light isolation/"primer" sets), slow eccentrics, pause in the
  shortened position, reduce load to feel the target, palpate the muscle, full ROM through the
  lengthened position.
- **Tempo:** controlled eccentrics and a brief peak-contraction pause reinforce the connection
  without needing heavier load.

## 3. Common Pitfalls
1. Using internal focus on heavy compounds (cuts force/safety). 2. Ego loading that recruits
synergists and kills the target focus. 3. Expecting MMC to replace progressive overload (it
complements — see `hypertrophy-biomechanics.md`). 4. Momentum/partial ROM. 5. Believing you
"can't grow without feeling it" — load + volume still rule.

## 4. Advanced Resources
