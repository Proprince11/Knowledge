# Diet & Macronutrient Planning

Body composition is governed first by **energy balance** (thermodynamics), then by
**macronutrient distribution** (which shapes *what* the energy surplus/deficit does to tissue),
then by micronutrients, food quality, and timing. The hierarchy matters: people optimize
timing/supplements while ignoring calories and protein — the variables that actually move
outcomes.

## 1. Technical Mechanisms
- **Energy balance:** `ΔStored energy = Intake − Expenditure`. **TDEE = BMR + TEF + EAT + NEAT.**
  NEAT (non-exercise activity) is the most variable component and adapts to under/overfeeding
  (metabolic adaptation) — why deficits stall.
- **Estimating needs:** BMR via Mifflin–St Jeor, ×activity for TDEE. These are *estimates*; the
  real meter is **tracking weight trend vs. intake over 2–3 weeks** and adjusting.
- **Protein** (4 kcal/g): preserves/builds lean mass, highest TEF (~20–30%), most satiating.
  **~1.6–2.2 g/kg/day**; toward the high end in a deficit. Driven by leucine/EAAs and total
  daily intake.
- **Fat** (9 kcal/g): essential (hormones, fat-soluble vitamins, cell membranes). Floor
  **~0.5–0.8 g/kg**; very low fat impairs hormones.
- **Carbohydrate** (4 kcal/g): primary training fuel (glycogen), performance and recovery;
  "non-essential" biochemically but highly useful. Fill remaining calories after protein/fat.
- **Fiber & micronutrients:** ~14 g/1000 kcal fiber; micronutrient sufficiency from varied whole
  foods underpins everything.

## 2. Application Frameworks
**Build a plan:** 1) Goal + rate (cut ~0.5–1%/wk bodyweight; lean bulk ~0.25–0.5%/wk). 2) Set
calories (deficit/surplus ~10–20% of TDEE). 3) Protein (1.6–2.2 g/kg). 4) Fat (≥0.5–0.8 g/kg).
5) Carbs = remainder. 6) Track trend, adjust.

**Adherence > optimality:** the best diet is the sustainable one. Flexible dieting ("IIFYM") with
a whole-food base (~80/20) beats rigid plans that collapse. **Protein + fiber + volume** maximize
satiety per calorie.

**Refeeds/diet breaks** can ease long deficits (psychological + leptin/NEAT). **Nutrient timing**
is a minor lever vs. totals: protein distributed across the day (~0.4 g/kg/meal), carbs around
training help performance.

## 3. Common Pitfalls
1. Ignoring calories while obsessing over timing/supplements. 2. Under-eating protein. 3. Fat too
low → hormonal issues. 4. Crash deficits → muscle loss, adaptation, rebound. 5. Not tracking the
weight *trend* (daily fluctuation ≠ fat change). 6. Liquid calories/underreporting. 7. Demonizing
single foods/macros. 8. Unsustainable rigidity. 9. Neglecting fiber/micros. 10. Expecting linear
progress (water/glycogen noise).
