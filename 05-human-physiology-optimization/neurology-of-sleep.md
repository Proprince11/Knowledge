# The Neurology of Sleep

Sleep is an **active, structured neurological process** essential for memory consolidation,
metabolic clearance, hormonal regulation, and immune function — not passive downtime. It is
governed by two interacting systems and progresses through architecturally distinct stages, each
doing different work.

## 1. Technical Mechanisms
- **Two-process model (Borbély):** **Process S** (homeostatic sleep pressure) builds with
  wakefulness, driven by **adenosine** accumulation (caffeine blocks adenosine receptors, masking
  pressure). **Process C** (circadian rhythm) is the ~24h clock in the **suprachiasmatic nucleus
  (SCN)**, entrained by light via melanopsin retinal cells; light suppresses **melatonin**
  (released by the pineal gland in darkness).
- **Sleep architecture (~90-min cycles):** **N1→N2→N3 (slow-wave/deep)** then **REM**. **Deep
  NREM** dominates early night (physical restoration, GH release, **glymphatic clearance** of
  metabolic waste incl. amyloid-β); **REM** lengthens toward morning (emotional processing,
  procedural memory, dreaming). Cutting sleep short preferentially robs REM.
- **Memory consolidation:** hippocampal-neocortical transfer during NREM (sharp-wave ripples,
  spindles); REM stabilizes emotional/procedural memory.
- **Hormonal/metabolic:** deprivation raises cortisol & ghrelin, lowers leptin (↑appetite),
  impairs insulin sensitivity and testosterone, and degrades reaction time/decision-making.

## 2. Application Frameworks (sleep hygiene)
- **Light discipline:** bright light early (anchor the SCN); dim/limit blue light at night.
  Morning sunlight is the strongest circadian anchor.
- **Consistency:** fixed sleep/wake times (even weekends) stabilize Process C — the
  highest-leverage habit.
- **Adenosine/caffeine:** avoid caffeine ~8–10h before bed (half-life ~5–6h).
- **Temperature:** a cool, dark room; core-temp drop initiates sleep.
- **Alcohol fragments sleep** and suppresses REM despite aiding sleep onset.
- **Wind-down + stimulus control:** bed = sleep only; if awake >~20 min, leave and return when
  sleepy (CBT-I principle).
- **Caffeine + 20-min nap** for an afternoon dip; avoid long/late naps that bleed Process S.

## 3. Common Pitfalls
1. Inconsistent schedule. 2. Nighttime bright/blue light. 3. Late caffeine/alcohol. 4. Equating
"hours" with quality (architecture matters). 5. "Catching up" on weekends (social jetlag).
6. Screens/work in bed. 7. Sacrificing sleep for training/work (counterproductive — it's the
master recovery variable for `hypertrophy-biomechanics.md` and cognition).
