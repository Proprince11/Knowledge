# Biohacking & Human Optimization

Biohacking is **self-experimentation toward measurable physiological/cognitive improvement**.
Done well, it's applied physiology with a feedback loop (measure → intervene → re-measure). Done
badly, it's expensive supplements and gadgets chasing effects that sleep, nutrition, and exercise
would deliver for free. The discipline is **prioritization + honest measurement**, not novelty.

## 1. Technical Mechanisms
- **The hierarchy (ROI-ordered):** the fundamentals dwarf everything else — **sleep**
  (`neurology-of-sleep.md`), **nutrition** (`diet-macronutrient-planning.md`), **exercise**
  (zone 2 + resistance), **sunlight/circadian light**, **stress regulation**, **social
  connection**. Gadgets/supplements are the last 5%.
- **Hormetic stressors:** mild stressors trigger adaptive overcompensation — **exercise**, **heat
  (sauna:** observational links to cardiovascular benefit), **cold exposure** (alertness, mood,
  brown-fat signaling — evidence mixed for fat loss), **fasting/time-restricted eating**
  (autophagy, insulin sensitivity — see `longevity.md`), **hypoxia/altitude**. Dose matters; too
  much stress is just damage.
- **Measurement tools:** wearables (HRV as autonomic-balance proxy, resting HR, sleep-stage
  estimates), CGMs (glucose response to food/stress), bloodwork panels (lipids, HbA1c, vitamin D,
  hormones, inflammatory markers). **Caveat:** consumer-device accuracy varies; track *trends*,
  not absolute single readings, and beware over-fitting to noisy data.
- **Nootropics:** caffeine + L-theanine is the best-supported, low-risk combo; most others have
  thin evidence and real risks — stimulant overuse disrupts the sleep that drives cognition
  (net-negative).

## 2. Application Frameworks
- **N-of-1 experiment design:** define one hypothesis + one metric → baseline (1–2 wks) → single
  intervention → re-measure → washout → control for confounders (sleep, stress, season). Change
  one variable at a time, or you learn nothing (mirrors
  `../03-computer-science-architecture/ai-ml-engineering.md` evaluation discipline).
- **Evidence-based stack (low-risk, high-ROI):** consistent sleep/wake + morning light; zone-2
  cardio + resistance training; protein/fiber-forward diet; sauna/cold *if enjoyed and
  tolerated*; stress practice (breathwork, meditation — measurable HRV/cortisol effects); creatine
  monohydrate (among the best-evidenced supplements for strength and cognition); vitamin D if
  deficient.
- **Safety gating:** get medical clearance for fasting/cold/heat if you have cardiovascular or
  metabolic conditions; don't stack unproven compounds; verify supplement third-party testing.

## 3. Common Pitfalls
1. Chasing gadgets/supplements while neglecting sleep/diet/exercise (inverting the hierarchy).
2. Over-fitting to noisy wearable data / health anxiety. 3. No controls or baselines —
unattributable results + placebo. 4. Dangerous extremes (prolonged fasting, ice baths,
megadosing). 5. Unproven nootropic stacks with interaction risk. 6. Stimulants wrecking sleep.
7. Survivorship bias / influencer marketing as "evidence." 8. Ignoring individual variation.
