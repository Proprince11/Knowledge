# Longevity & Healthspan

The goal is **healthspan** (years of healthy function), not merely lifespan. Modern geroscience
treats aging as a set of modifiable biological processes ("hallmarks of aging") whose
deceleration may compress morbidity. The honest framing: the **fundamentals have strong human
evidence**; most pharmacological/supplement "longevity" interventions are **preclinical or
unproven** in humans.

## 1. Technical Mechanisms
- **The Hallmarks of Aging (López-Otín et al.):** genomic instability, telomere attrition,
  epigenetic alterations, loss of proteostasis, **deregulated nutrient sensing**, mitochondrial
  dysfunction, **cellular senescence**, stem-cell exhaustion, altered intercellular communication,
  chronic inflammation ("inflammaging"), disabled autophagy, dysbiosis. These interact; targeting
  one often affects others.
- **Nutrient-sensing pathways:** **mTOR** (growth vs. repair), **AMPK** (energy stress, activated
  by exercise/caloric restriction), **sirtuins** (NAD⁺-dependent), **IGF-1/insulin**. Caloric
  restriction extends lifespan across many species (robust in animals; human data favor
  metabolic-health benefits) partly via these pathways and **autophagy**.
- **Cellular senescence & senolytics:** senescent "zombie" cells secrete inflammatory factors
  (SASP); clearing them improves healthspan in mice — **senolytics** are an active but
  human-unproven frontier.
- **The big killers set the priorities:** cardiovascular disease, cancer, neurodegeneration,
  metabolic disease. Longevity ≈ delaying these — so **cardiometabolic and muscular health**
  dominate the actionable agenda.

## 2. Application Frameworks (evidence-tiered)
**Tier 1 — strong human evidence (do these):**
- **Don't smoke;** limit alcohol.
- **Exercise** — the closest thing to a longevity drug: **VO₂max and muscle mass/strength are
  among the strongest predictors of all-cause mortality.** Zone-2 cardio + VO₂max work +
  resistance training + maintaining muscle into older age.
- **Sleep** (`neurology-of-sleep.md`) and **stress/social connection** (loneliness is a mortality
  risk factor).
- **Diet:** whole-food, fiber- and protein-adequate, minimally processed; maintain healthy body
  composition; protect against sarcopenia with protein + lifting.
- **Medical:** manage blood pressure, ApoB/lipids, glucose; vaccinations; screening; preserve
  metabolic health.

**Tier 2 — promising but unproven/contextual in humans:** caloric restriction / time-restricted
eating (metabolic benefits clearer than lifespan), specific supplements; treat as experimental.

**Tier 3 — speculative / research-stage:** rapamycin, metformin (TAME trial ongoing), NAD⁺
precursors, senolytics, "epigenetic reprogramming." Not established for healthy-human life
extension; risks and unknowns remain.

> **Principle:** capture the large, proven Tier-1 gains *first*. They outweigh every speculative
> compound — and most "longevity influencers" invert this.

## 3. Common Pitfalls
1. Chasing Tier-3 molecules while neglecting Tier-1 (exercise, sleep, not smoking, cardiometabolic
management). 2. Confusing animal/cell results with human proof. 3. Losing muscle/VO₂max with age
(top modifiable mortality risks). 4. Marketing/anecdote over evidence; expensive "anti-aging"
supplements. 5. Ignoring mental health/social connection. 6. Extreme interventions with unknown
long-term risk. 7. Optimizing biomarkers while ignoring hard outcomes.

## 4. Advanced Resources
- López-Otín et al., *Hallmarks of Aging* (Cell, 2013/2023 update); Attia, *Outlive*; Sinclair,
  *Lifespan* (read critically); peer-reviewed VO₂max/strength–mortality studies (e.g., Mandsager
  2018); TAME trial info.
