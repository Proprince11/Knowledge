# Financial Literacy

Personal finance is **simple in principle, hard in behavior**: spend less than you earn, insure
against catastrophe, and let compounding work over decades. The math is arithmetic; the
difficulty is psychological. This file lays out the durable principles — compounding, the wealth
equation, risk, and tax-advantaged investing — that hold regardless of market noise.

## 1. Technical Mechanisms
- **Compound interest (the central force):** `FV = PV·(1 + r)^n`. Growth is *exponential* in time,
  so **time in the market** dominates. **Rule of 72:** money doubles in ≈ `72 / r%` years (at 8%,
  ~9 years). Starting a decade earlier can out-earn contributing far more later — compounding
  rewards the early and the patient.
- **The wealth equation:** `Wealth = (Income − Spending) compounded over time`. The lever is the
  **gap** (savings rate), not income alone — high earners with a zero gap stay broke. Your
  **savings rate** is the single biggest determinant of time-to-financial-independence.
- **Inflation:** erodes purchasing power (`real return ≈ nominal − inflation`). Cash "safe" in
  nominal terms loses value in real terms — why long-horizon money must be invested, not hoarded.
- **Risk & return:** higher expected return demands higher risk/volatility. **Diversification**
  (across assets, geographies, time) reduces *unsystematic* risk for free — "the only free lunch."
  **Asset allocation** (stocks/bonds/etc.) drives most of a portfolio's return variance, far more
  than individual picks.
- **Market efficiency:** markets are largely (not perfectly) efficient; **most active managers
  underperform low-cost index funds after fees** over the long run (SPIVA data). Fees and taxes
  are the controllable drags — minimize them.
- **The behavior gap:** investors underperform their own funds by buying high (greed) and selling
  low (fear). Temperament beats intelligence in investing.

## 2. Application Frameworks
- **The ordered priority stack (general principle):**
  1. **Budget + emergency fund** (3–6 months expenses) — insulates against forced selling/debt
     spirals.
  2. **Eliminate high-interest debt** (credit cards at ~20% = a guaranteed, huge negative return —
     pay first).
  3. **Capture any employer retirement match** — an instant ~100% return; never leave it.
  4. **Tax-advantaged investing** (retirement accounts) in **low-cost, broad, diversified index
     funds**; automate contributions.
  5. **Insurance** against catastrophe (health, liability, life if dependents) — protect the
     downside.
  6. Further taxable investing; advanced goals.
- **Core investing principles:** start early, automate, diversify broadly, keep costs low, hold
  through volatility, and **don't try to time the market** (time *in* > timing). Rebalance
  periodically.
- **Behavioral guardrails (cf. `../09-humanities-master-skills/behavioral-logic.md`):** automate
  to remove emotion; ignore the news cycle; pre-commit to a written plan/policy statement;
  understand loss aversion will scream at exactly the wrong moments.
- **For creators/entrepreneurs:** separate business and personal finances; understand **cash flow**
  (the killer of businesses), **CAC/LTV** (`../01-monetization-digital-empires/`), runway, and
  taxes; pay yourself and invest the surplus.

## 3. Common Pitfalls
1. Lifestyle inflation (spending rising with income → zero gap). 2. Carrying high-interest debt
while investing. 3. No emergency fund → forced selling/debt at the worst time. 4. Trying to
time/beat the market; stock-picking and chasing hot performance. 5. High-fee active funds/
advisors eroding returns. 6. Panic-selling in downturns, FOMO-buying in bubbles (the behavior gap).
7. Under-diversification (single stocks, employer stock, one asset). 8. Ignoring inflation on cash.
9. Leaving employer match on the table. 10. No plan / no automation (relying on willpower).
11. Get-rich-quick schemes, leverage, and "can't-lose" tips (red flags — cf.
`../09-humanities-master-skills/influence-and-dark-psychology-defense.md`).
