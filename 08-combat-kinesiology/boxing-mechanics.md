# Boxing Mechanics

Boxing is **applied kinetic-chain physics under time pressure** — generating maximal force at the
fist while remaining defensively sound, balanced, and able to repeat it. Punching power is a
coordination skill, not a strength contest: lighter, technically superior punchers routinely
out-hit stronger novices because they sequence the chain better.

## 1. Technical Mechanisms
- **Power generation (ground up):** force originates in the **legs pushing the ground** (GRF) →
  **hip rotation** → **trunk rotation** → **shoulder** → **arm extension** → fist. The
  proximal-to-distal sequence (see `kinetic-chain-optimization.md`) converts whole-body mass into
  fist velocity. **Effective mass** behind the punch (how much body mass is "connected" at impact)
  matters as much as speed.
- **Impact physics:** `Impulse = Force × time = Δmomentum`; `KE = ½mv²` — velocity is squared, so
  hand speed dominates energy delivered. A rigid, aligned wrist/fist at impact transmits force
  efficiently (a loose joint leaks energy and injures the puncher).
- **The punches:**
  - **Jab:** lead-hand, range-finder/setup; speed and timing over power.
  - **Cross:** rear-hand straight; maximal hip/trunk rotation + rear-leg drive → the power punch.
  - **Hook:** horizontal, rotational; hip-and-pivot driven; devastating at close range.
  - **Uppercut:** vertical, leg-drive + slight trunk extension; inside fighting.
- **Stance & balance:** base width, weight distribution, and a stable center of gravity enable
  power *and* mobility; punching off-balance forfeits both power and defense.
- **Foot pivot:** the lead/rear foot pivots to allow full hip rotation — "screwing into the
  ground." Power that isn't anchored by footwork can't be delivered.

## 2. Application Frameworks
- **Power development:** medicine-ball rotational throws, plyometric push-ups, explosive
  rotational work, and resisted punch drills train the SSC and proximal-to-distal sequence; heavy
  strength supports it but doesn't replace coordination.
- **Mechanics checklist for a hard cross:** rear foot drives + pivots → hips rotate → trunk
  follows → shoulder rotates → arm extends last → fist lands with a braced wrist → recover to
  guard. *Power is in the sequence and the return, not the arm.*
- **Defense is offense's twin:** head movement (slips, rolls), guard, footwork (distance
  management), and the **return to stance** after punching. Every punch should end with you
  defensively reset.
- **Conditioning:** boxing is glycolytic + aerobic; intervals matching round structure, plus
  neck/core for force transfer and resilience.
- **Repeatability:** technique that holds under fatigue wins; drilling builds the motor pattern so
  it survives round 12.

## 3. Common Pitfalls
1. **Arm punching** — no leg drive/hip rotation → no power. 2. Telegraphing (loading up,
  windup). 3. Dropping the non-punching hand / not returning to guard → countered. 4. Poor
  wrist/fist alignment → energy leak + hand injury. 5. Punching off-balance or flat-footed (no
  pivot). 6. Holding breath (exhale sharply on impact for bracing). 7. Overcommitting / poor
  weight management between offense and defense. 8. Neglecting defense and footwork for power.
9. Ignoring head-trauma risk in sparring volume.
